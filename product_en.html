We deliver the best services to our clients with all these products that are backed by many years of experience and technology development.

  • Silicone x Keypads

    Silicone keypad products can usually be backlit, are non-toxic and very eco-friendly. They feel good to the touch, can be combined with other material such as plastic or metal and have a long lifespan. JS Group Ltd. have more than five years of successful experience manufacturing high-performance silicone keypads for equipment such as cell phones, game controllers, and remote controls.

  • Silicone x Eco-Friendly Products

    The main component of silicone is silicon dioxide, an amorphous machine-made compound created from sodium silicate and sulfuric acid contained in silica. Because the toxic components in silicone are fully released and extracted during the manufacturing process and that silicone can withstand a high temperature of 200 degrees, it can be made into products such as cup rings and eco-friendly silicone saran wrap.

  • Silicone x Women’s and Children’s Products

    Due to its non-toxic and eco-friendly nature, many manufacturers utilize silicone to fabricate breast-mimicking silicone pacifiers, baby/toddler feeding bowls, baby/toddler training chopsticks, silicone bibs, and baby/toddler collapsible bowls which all share the characteristics of being lightweight and convenient to use and store.

  • Silicone x Floor Mats

    Silicone mixed with other compounds have high tensile and tear strength which provide cushioning and shock absorbing functions. They can effectively block external or internal vibration, making the operation of the inner part of a machine smoother and protecting the equipment from wear and tear due to accumulated vibration over a long period of time.

  • Silicone x Pads

    The non-slip and shock absorbing properties of silicone help maintain the original elasticity and shape of silicone pads. It is a good cushioning material because the shock absorbing performance is not diminished even after a long-duration pressure test.

  • Silicone x Gaskets

    Silicones are polymers of organic and inorganic components, they have a very high thermal stability which keeps them stable between -100 °C to 250 °C. They are also non-stick, low in chemical reactivity and gas permeable, which are the reasons why they are widely used as gaskets for machinery.

  • Silicone x Medical Products

    Silicone not only meets FDA requirements, it is also an ideal material for manufacturing and processing. Because silicone is heat-resistant, flame-retardant, non-reactive, weather-resistant, nonconductive and radiation-resistant, it can be made into medical device parts, accessories or wearable medical products.

  • Silicone x Jack Covers

    Rubber jack covers are resistant to cold, high temperatures, ozone, oils, and aging, and nonconductive. They can be used in electronic equipment and general consumer goods.

  • Rubber x Shock Absorbing Mats

    Rubber is a polymer of high molecular weight which can be stretched more than twice its original length and quickly returns to its original size. Rubber can be generally divided into natural rubber and synthetic rubber. They have excellent elasticity, strong tensile strength and are abrasion-resistant and can be used to produce shock absorbing mats.

  • Rubber x Shock Absorbers

    Rubber has a dynamic load-bearing and deformation capacity. It can block vibration noise and is highly shockproof. It is suitable for the cushioning design of all types of mechanical equipment, including shock absorber parts of electronic equipment, control panels, air compressors, and all types of mechanical equipment.

  • Rubber x Oil Seals

    As the name indicates, oil seals’ primary function is to maintain the pressure within a system and prevent the hydraulic oil from leaking out and dust from moving in. They are made of synthetic rubber or alloys that are crush, abrasion, and heat resistant and often used in industrial equipment, motor vehicle parts and all types of transmission equipment.

  • Rubber x O-rings

    An O-ring is a circular mechanical gasket and a doughnut-shaped elastomer with a round cross section. It is usually seated in a groove and compressed between two or more parts during assembly, creating a seal at the interface. Due to the low price, easy manufacturing, reliable performance, and simple mounting requirements, O-rings are the most common seals used in machine design.

  • Rubber x Industrial Parts

    Many types of synthetic rubbers have low gas permeability and good resistance to heat, aging, weather, and ozone. They are also nonconductive or resistant to mechanical abrasion with a strong tensile strength which help maintain the elasticity even in high hardness. They are often used as industrial, electrical, and electronic parts.

  • Rubber x Automotive Parts

    Rubber has a very long history of being used in automotive parts. Since synthetic rubbers are resistant to oils, heat, abrasion, acids, and alkalis, highly elastic, non-toxic, shock-absorbing, anti-static and nonconductive, they work very well in high-speed vehicles.

  • Rubber x Grips

    Many developers apply the properties of rubber to grip manufacturing because of the high physical performance in tear, abrasion, and weather resistance, as well as zero waste created during the manufacturing process. The surface texture is soft, slip resistant and feels similar to human skin, creating a comfortable experience for the users who grasp or hold the products.

  • Rubber x Weatherproof Products

    Weather resistance is the ability of materials such as coating, building plastics, or rubber products to withstand the damages caused by weather elements, such as light, heat, cold, wind, rain, and bacteria, when installed outdoors. Weather-resistant rubber products can extend the lifespan of the products used outdoors and JS Group Ltd. have successful experience with them.
